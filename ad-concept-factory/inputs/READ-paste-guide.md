# This is where you paste your inputs

> This folder is the system's input. You fill the three files once, then the system reads from here on every run.
> The more real and rich the input, the more accurate the output. Made-up input = made-up pattern.

## Three files

### 1) `winning-ads.md`
Your brand's best-performing ads. How you know a "winner":
- If you run ads: your best-selling / longest-running / lowest-cost ads in your ad account.
- If you do not run ads: your organic posts with the most engagement (likes, saves, comments).
Fill this template for each ad:

```text
## Ad 1
Hook (first sentence): <the ad's opening sentence>
Promise / offer: <what it promises>
Format: single image / video / carousel
Performance note: <e.g. best for 8 months / best-selling / running 3 months>
Text: <the full ad text>

---

## Ad 2
...
```

### 2) `customer-reviews.md`
Real customer reviews. Marketplace, site reviews, DMs, email. Copy and paste, no editing. At least 8-10 is good. Take the praise and the hesitations alike: reviews with an objection are worth gold.

```text
- "<customer review verbatim>"
- "<customer review verbatim>"
- "<review with a hesitation/objection>"
```

### 3) `liked-comments.md`
The comments that got the MOST LIKES on social media (Instagram, X, TikTok), on your posts or your industry's. These are the map of the viral angle: humor, righteous anger, "wow" moment. The most-liked comment is the crowd thinking out loud.

```text
- "<most-liked comment>"  (likes: ~high)
- "<most-liked comment>"
```

## Tips
- Start with at least 3-4 winning ads, 8-10 customer reviews, 3-5 liked comments. A pattern does not come from a single ad.
- Do not edit the reviews; the customer's own sentence is the strongest hook.
- Do not delete this guide file; the system only reads the three input files, this file can stay.
