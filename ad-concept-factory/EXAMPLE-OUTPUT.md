## A) Input summary + extracted pattern

4 winning ads, 10 customer reviews, 3 liked comments read. The hook type that keeps landing: pain/question ("when was your supermarket coffee roasted", best for 8 months) + sensory (aroma, "the smell of your home changes") + number (4 per cup, 90 outside). Common promise: freshness and the roast date being printed on the pack (removing risk concretely). Common emotion: relief/trust + smart spending. Most common objection: price ("it felt a bit expensive at first"); the best customer sentence that refutes it: "per cup it comes cheaper than the supermarket". Second objection: fear of commitment/cancellation; the sentence that refutes it: "i had a fear that cancelling would be hard, it was one click". Viral angle (most liked): "i thought i loved coffee, turns out i loved stale coffee" and "why is a roast date being printed such a rare thing". The working logic: remind them of the pain of staleness, prove freshness concretely with the roast date, break the price down per cup, and remove the commitment fear with one-click cancellation.

> Anti-invention note: for numbers/offers not in the input, a placeholder was left. Değirmen Coffee's input has NO total customer count, star rating, years in business, or full subscription price. Those lines were marked with `[...]`, not invented. The quotes used are only from the real input reviews. Known real numbers (20% on the first box, 4 per cup, 90 outside, "roasted three days ago") were used because they came from the winning ad / review input.
