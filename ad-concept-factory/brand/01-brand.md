# 01 · Brand

> The unchanging knowledge behind the concepts: who, for whom, what we sell, what we are not, proof.
> The system builds the 50 concepts on this file. Fill it once, it rarely changes.
> If you already set up Brand Memory, Ad Spy, or the Carousel Generator, this file is the same as the 01 file there, copy it.

## Who / what we do
In one sentence, what you do, what you sell.
Example: "A small online roaster selling freshly roasted single-origin coffee: a monthly subscription and a one-time pack."

## For whom (target audience)
Who you speak to. Age, pain, lifestyle, reason to buy. Concepts are written to this person.
Example: "people who want good coffee at home, tired of supermarket coffee, 25-45; filter/moka/espresso drinkers who treat coffee as a ritual."

## What you sell (offers + real numbers)
Write your offer/product names and, IF you have them, your real offers. The system never invents a discount/offer that is not here.
- Offer 1: <name> · <price range / campaign>
- Offer 2: <name> · <price range / campaign>
- Your first-order / shipping / cancellation / return terms (write the real one if you have it, leave blank if not).

## Why you (differentiation)
The thing only you can say, that your competitor cannot. The "promise" side of the concepts feeds from here.
Example: "we roast when the order comes in, the roast date is printed on the pack; supermarket coffee was roasted months ago."

## What you are NOT
The boundary. Reading these lines, the system will not blindly copy the exaggerated language in a winning ad or a review.
Example: "no 'world's best' hype. no 'last 24 hours' fake-urgency language. we do not sell capsules/instant coffee."

## Proof / trust elements
Numbers, references, guarantees, duration. On the "rationale/proof" side of a concept, credibility comes from these.
The system never puts a made-up number; it uses whatever is on this line, and leaves a placeholder if there is none.
Example: "since 2022 · 12,000+ boxes shipped · most subscribers stay 6 months+ · cancel anytime with one click · 4.9 rating."

## Industry / competitor note (optional)
Which industry you are in, who you compete with. General framing; the winning ad and review inputs are in separate files (`inputs/`).
Example: "third-wave coffee; competitors are both big supermarket brands and small boutique roasters."
