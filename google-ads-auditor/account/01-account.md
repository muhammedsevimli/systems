# 01 · Account (what you sell, for whom, your targets)

> Fill in this file with your own business. The system compares each search term against this to make the "does it fit the business, is it wasted" distinction. If you leave it empty, the system cannot separate the relevant from the irrelevant.
> Filled-in example: `account/EXAMPLE-01-account.md`.

## What you sell
Your product/service, in one or two sentences. The system reads this and says "is this search looking for my product".

## For whom (ideal customer)
Who buys. The person, the need, the purchase intent. (E.g. "the end user looking for a quality product for a gift or their own kitchen.")

## Target ROAS / CPA
- Target ROAS (how many times the ad spend should come back): e.g. `4x`.
- Target CPA (how much a conversion should cost you at most): e.g. `$15`.
(If you do not know, leave it empty; the system still finds the wasted spend but cannot comment on budget/ROAS.)

## Which searches FIT you (relevant intent)
The search types the person who actually wants to buy your product would write. E.g. "product name + purchase intent".

## What you are NOT / which searches are wasted (irrelevant intent)
The system counts these as wasted. Fill in based on your own business. Typical wasted intents:
- informational search ("how to make", "how to clean", "what is it for") · does not buy, wants to learn.
- DIY / do it yourself ("... painting", "... recipe", "make ... at home").
- competitor or other brand name (not looking for your product).
- "cheap / second hand / free" · contradicts your positioning.
- a segment you do not serve: <wholesale / raw material / corporate / dealership ...>. Write your own "I do not do this" lines here.

## Applied fixes (write-back · the system fills this in)
Starts empty. When you apply a fix, the system writes it here with the date. Next month, when it audits the new report, the system reads this, does not suggest the same thing again, and compares the result.
(Date · what you did · which campaign/term · expected effect)
