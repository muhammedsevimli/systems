> Fictional business: Oak Handcrafted Wood.

## What you sell
I sell handmade wooden kitchen products: cutting boards, serving boards, wooden spoons. Made from oak, walnut, and olive wood, produced one by one in the workshop. Mid-to-high segment, gift-friendly. Online sales, shipping nationwide.

## For whom (ideal customer)
The end user looking for a quality, long-lasting product for their own kitchen or a special gift. Looks at material and craftsmanship, not price. Usually buys a single product between $50 and $150.

## Target ROAS / CPA
- Target ROAS: `4x` (every $1 I spend should bring at least $4 in revenue).
- Target CPA: `$15` (a sale should cost me at most $15 in ad spend).

## Which searches FIT you (relevant intent)
- product + purchase: "wooden cutting board", "handmade cutting board", "walnut serving board", "olive wood cutting board", "oak cutting board".
- brand searches: "oak handcrafted wood", "oak cutting board" (ones with my brand name).

## What you are NOT / which searches are wasted (irrelevant intent)
- **informational:** "how to make", "how to clean", "how to polish" · someone wanting to learn, not to buy.
- **DIY / do it yourself:** "wooden spoon painting", "make a wooden board yourself" · I sell the finished product, not material.
- **cheap / second hand:** "cheap plastic cutting board", "second hand wooden plate" · against my segment, I do not sell plastic.
- **competitor / other brand:** another brand name like "ikea cutting board" · not looking for my product.
- **raw material / lumber:** "oak lumber prices", "wood raw material" · I sell the finished product, not lumber.
- **a segment I do not serve:** I do not do wholesale. "wholesale cutting boards" is not my target customer even if it brings a single sale, low return.
