# 01 · Profile (the field you care about, what you can build, your limits)

> This is the ONLY thing the system wants from you. You write the field; the system scans demand on its own.
> This file is also what "filters the idea for you": the system reads every idea against the fields and limits here.
> Fill in the angle brackets with your own. `you/EXAMPLE-01-profile.md` has a filled-in version (a fictional operator) if you want to see the shape first.

## The field / niche you care about (where the scan starts)
The system scans demand in this field. Be specific; one field or a few.
- <e.g. local appointment businesses: hair salons, barbers, beauty studios, clinics>
- <e.g. small shops selling through Instagram and handmade sellers>
- <e.g. small ecommerce operations: orders, returns, shipping>

## Your region / market (criterion 4 is scored against this)
The country and language you would sell into, and how you would actually get paid there. The system scores "market fit for your region" against THIS, not against a market it assumes.
- Country / region: <e.g. UK · English speaking>
- Language of your customers: <e.g. English>
- How you can collect payment: <e.g. Stripe, yes / not set up yet>
- Local reality worth knowing: <e.g. most of these businesses run on WhatsApp, not email>

## Who you are / what you do
In one sentence: <e.g. I build tools that make small businesses' digital work easier, and I build them with AI.>

## Which fields you know (idea accuracy comes from here)
The fields you are genuinely inside, whose jargon you know, whose pain you have lived. The system gives ideas in these fields a higher "fit for you".
- Field 1: <e.g. ecommerce, shipping, returns operations>
- Field 2: <e.g. local trades, appointment-taking businesses (salon, clinic, studio)>
- Field 3: <e.g. content production, social media management>

## What you can build (your own building ability)
The system reads feasibility against this. Be honest.
- Your coding level: <e.g. I do not write code, I build with AI.>
- Structures you are comfortable with: <e.g. a simple web panel, forms, tables, automated email, a WhatsApp or Telegram bot.>
- Structures you struggle with or avoid: <e.g. mobile apps, payment infrastructure, live video processing.>
- Hours you can give per week: <e.g. 8-10 hours.>
- Your setup budget: <e.g. low monthly, free and cheap tiers first.>

## What you can sell (your collection reality)
- Can you take payments: <e.g. yes, with Stripe / not yet.>
- Who you want to sell to: <e.g. a small business that can pay $20-60 a month.>

## Your limits (the things you said you will not do)
The system uses these to eliminate an idea or drop it to the bottom.
- <e.g. I will not touch health data or diagnosis (regulation).>
- <e.g. I will not build a straight clone of a competitor; if I cannot differentiate, I stay out.>
- <e.g. I do not want work that needs constant live support.>
- <e.g. I do not want work that depends on a single customer and does not scale.>

## Why you (your unfair advantage)
Anyone can see the same idea; what makes you different. Your industry contacts, an audience you already have, deep experience in one field.
- <e.g. I am in a 200-person local business group chat, I would find the first 10 customers there.>
