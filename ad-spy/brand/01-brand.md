# 01 · Brand

> The unchanging information behind the concepts: who, for whom, what we sell, what we are not, proof.
> The system builds the 10 concepts from this file. Fill it once, it rarely changes.
> If you already set up a brand memory or carousel generator, this file is the same as the 01 file there, copy it.

## Who / what we do
What you do, what you sell, in one sentence.
Example: "A small e-commerce brand selling home textiles: duvet covers, towels, coverlets."

## For whom (target audience)
Who you speak to. Age, pain, lifestyle, reason for buying. Concepts are written for this person.
Example: "people 25-45 who want to refresh their home, looking for quality but affordable products, mostly shopping online."

## What you sell (offers + real numbers)
Write your product/offer names and, IF you have them, your real offers. The system does not invent a discount/offer that is not here.
- Offer 1: <name> · <price range / campaign>
- Offer 2: <name> · <price range / campaign>
- Your first-order / shipping / return terms (write the real one if you have it, otherwise leave blank).

## Why you (differentiation)
The thing only you can say, that your competitor cannot. The "promise" part of the concepts feeds from here.
Example: "not wholesale, each product passed one-by-one quality control; cotton that does not stiffen with washing."

## What you are NOT
The boundary. When the system reads these lines it will not blindly copy the competitor's angle.
Example: "we do not do cheap-race pricing. we do not use 'last 24 hours' fake urgency language. we do not make exaggerated 'hotel quality' promises."

## Proof / trust signals
Numbers, references, guarantees, track record. Credibility on the concept's "proof" line comes from these.
The system does NOT put made-up numbers; it uses whatever is on this line, and leaves a placeholder if there is nothing.
Example: "since 2021 · 8,000+ orders · 15-day no-questions returns · 4.8 store rating."

## Industry / competitor note (optional)
Which industry you are in, who you compete with. The competitor list is in a separate file (`03-competitors.md`); this is the general frame.
Example: "mid-segment home textiles; competitors are both large brands and small boutique e-commerce shops."
