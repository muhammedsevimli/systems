### Winning candidates and why

- **Pamuklu Ad 1 (6 months) · strongest.** No one pays for a losing ad for half a year. This structure, opening with a pain sentence and closing with a risk-free return, means it converts as money is spent. Its hook hits exactly our target audience's pain (the group that bought cheap and regretted it).
- **Pamuklu Ad 2 (5 months) · durability hook.** Running for a long time shows that the "buy once, use for years" message solves the price objection.
- **Yumağ Ad 4 (5 months) · desire hook.** Runs long, strong. But it contains fake urgency ("limited stock, do not miss it"); that is our banned zone, we will take its logic and not its language.
- **Yumağ Ad 5 (4 months) · benefit + social proof.** The concrete benefit "softens with washing" and the "thousands of customers" proof convert together.
- Ads 3 and 6 are a few weeks old, still in the test phase; they are not the backbone of the pattern but we note the gift and novelty angles as secondary signals.

## B) Extracted pattern (one paragraph)

The ads running for months all feed from the same place: they speak to someone who once bought cheap textiles and, after they wore out quickly, regretted it. The hook type is usually pain or nostalgia, the common offer type is risk-removal and durability (a return promise, long life, not breaking down with washing), and the common emotion is avoiding regret and the relief of "choosing the right one this time". Social proof (thousands of customers) and a concrete product benefit (long-staple cotton, no stiffening) are the second layer supporting this emotion. In short, the logic that clearly works is: remind of the pain, remove the risk, show the durability concretely. This logic overlaps exactly with Sırma's real differentiation (one-by-one quality control, long-staple cotton, 15-day no-questions returns, a hand-written note), so we dress that logic in your voice rather than the competitor's words.

## C) 10 concepts

### Concept 1 · Threadbare regret
- hook: a cheap duvet goes threadbare in two washes, you know it. ours is long-staple cotton, it does not stiffen with washing.
- format: video · in the first 3 seconds an old threadbare duvet, then a smooth Sırma duvet stroked by hand.
- promise/offer: long-staple cotton double duvet, 15-day no-questions return.
- cta: come to the site, look at the texture.
- which competitor logic it came from: Pamuklu's 6-month pain hook and risk-removal offer.
- why it can land: the target already bought cheap and regretted it, the pain sentence stops the scroll; the return promise removes the decision risk.

### Concept 2 · Decide in 15 days
- hook: no one loves a duvet without washing it first. use it 15 days, if you do not like it send it back.
- format: single image · a duvet spread on the bed, a plain "15-day no-questions return" badge in the corner.
- promise/offer: 15-day no-questions return, free shipping on the first order.
- cta: place your order, try it with ease.
- which competitor logic it came from: Pamuklu's risk-free trial logic; the competitor says 30 days, we say the real 15.
- why it can land: it lowers the fear of buying and builds trust with an understated, real promise.

### Concept 3 · Does not stiffen with washing
- hook: a good duvet does not stiffen with washing, it gets more used to your skin.
- format: video · the texture of the same duvet after the first and the thirtieth wash, side by side.
- promise/offer: long-staple cotton duvet, in the 899 to 1499 range.
- cta: come to the site, see the fabric up close.
- which competitor logic it came from: Yumağ's 4-month "softens with washing" benefit hook.
- why it can land: it pairs a concrete product benefit with a pain everyone knows (a stiffening duvet).

### Concept 4 · Good cotton is bought once
- hook: you buy good cotton once, you use it for years.
- format: single image · an aged but still solid duvet, in warm home light.
- promise/offer: long-staple cotton double duvet; every product passes one-by-one quality control.
- cta: come to the site, pick one.
- which competitor logic it came from: Pamuklu's 5-month durability and nostalgia hook.
- why it can land: the "buy once" message turns price into an investment, and the long-life promise overlaps with your real differentiation.

### Concept 5 · We packed it with our own hands

### Concept 9 · We will not rush you
- hook: we will not rush you. look as long as you want, compare, then decide.

- why it can land: it offers relief to a buyer tired of pressure and reinforces trust with the return promise.

### Concept 10 · Know who you bought from
- hook: your order is packed by us, not a factory. we look at every box one by one.
- format: video · the quality-control and packing moment, hands visible.
- promise/offer: one-by-one quality control on every product, a hand-written note in every order, free shipping.
- cta: come to the site, see who you bought from.
- which competitor logic it came from: the personal touch the big competitor Yumağ cannot give; a gap angle.
- why it can land: small-brand trust and transparency stand out in a crowded market and are remembered.

## D) Platform note

All the hook and CTA text above is written in the "you" form for Instagram / Facebook, usable directly.

LinkedIn measured-register version: shift the address and verbs to the more measured tone. Examples:
- Concept 1: "you bought a cheap duvet, it went threadbare in two washes, sounds familiar. ours is long-staple cotton, it does not stiffen with washing."
- Concept 2: "it is hard to love a duvet without washing it first. use it 15 days, if you do not like it send it back."
- Concept 9: "we will not rush you. look as long as you want, compare, then decide."
- Concept 10: "your order is packed by us, not a factory. we look at every box one by one."
The CTAs stay measured too: "come to the site", "read the reviews", "dm us". The tone stays clear, warm and understated; on LinkedIn, ease off the emotion a touch and bring concrete proof like quality control and durability to the front.
