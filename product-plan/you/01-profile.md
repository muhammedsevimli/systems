# 01 · Profile (who you are, who you are building for, your limits)

> The system reorders every feature against this file. This is the file that turns the plan from "generally correct" into YOUR plan.
> If this file is empty the system gives you a generic product plan, not a plan for you.
> The example below is filled in (a fictional operator). Change it for yourself.

## Who you are / what you do
In one sentence: <e.g. I am a one person studio that builds simple websites and small digital tools for local businesses.>

## Who you are building for (the niche)
Who will use the product. The clearer you write it, the more accurate the plan.
- <e.g. neighbourhood businesses: bakeries, salons, cafes, boutiques. places with 1 to 5 staff.>
- <e.g. the owners are not technical, they check things on a phone, they do not read long dashboards.>
- <e.g. businesses that can pay 20 to 100 dollars a month.>

## Your region and how you collect payment
The plan changes with this. Write it plainly.
- Region / market: <e.g. one city, English speaking market.>
- How you collect payment today: <e.g. invoice and bank transfer, yearly up front. no card subscriptions yet.>
- What payment habit your customers have: <e.g. they dislike monthly card subscriptions, they prefer paying once a year.>

## This audience's reality (this is what shapes the plan)
The target product's audience and yours may not be the same. Write the difference here.
- <e.g. my customers' sites get 500 to 5000 visitors a month, not much.>
- <e.g. my customer does not ask "how many people visited", they ask "how many people called me".>
- <e.g. they do not read a report, they want a single screenshot in a message.>

## What you can build (your own build power)
The system cuts the v1 scope against this. Be honest.
- Your coding level: <e.g. I do not write code, I build with Claude Code.>
- Structures you are comfortable with: <e.g. simple web panel, form, table, automatic email, message notification.>
- Structures you struggle with or avoid: <e.g. mobile apps, complex payment infrastructure, real time big data.>
- Hours you can give per week: <e.g. 8 to 10 hours.>
- Your build budget: <e.g. low per month, free or cheap tiers first.>

## What you can sell (your collection reality)
- Can you take payment: <e.g. for now bank transfer, payment infrastructure later.>
- How many customers can you reach: <e.g. I already have 20 small business customers.>

## Your limits (the things you said you will not do)
The system takes any feature that hits these limits out of v1 or drops it into the STAY OUT bucket.
- <e.g. I do not build anything that collects personal data or matches identities.>
- <e.g. I do not build a one to one copy product, if I am not differentiating I am not going in.>
- <e.g. I do not want work that needs live support around the clock.>
- <e.g. I do not go into regulated fields like health, finance or legal.>

## Why you (your unfair advantage)
Anyone can build the same product, what makes you different.
- <e.g. 20 businesses are already my customers, I do not have to go find the first users.>
- <e.g. I know exactly what these businesses ask, I speak their language.>
