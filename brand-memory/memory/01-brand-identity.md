# 01 · Brand Identity

> This file is the UNCHANGING core of your brand. Who you are, who you speak to, what you sell.
> It rarely changes. Review it once a year. Every line here is the reference behind
> every piece of text the system produces.

## Who
What <brand/business name> is, and what it does in one sentence.
Example: "A boutique food brand that brings olives and olive oil from the grower straight to city families."

## For whom (target audience)
Who you sell to. Age, city, income, pain, lifestyle.
Example: "30-45, living in a big city, pays for quality food, reads labels, families."

## What you sell (offers)
Product/service list + price range + who it fits.
- Offer 1: <name> · <price> · <for whom>
- Offer 2: <name> · <price> · <for whom>

## Why you (differentiation)
The thing your competitor cannot say, only you can.
Example: "We show which village and which harvest every bottle came from, with a QR code."

## What you are NOT
A boundary so you are not misread. When the system reads these lines it does not produce the wrong tone.
Example: "We are not a cheap supermarket brand. We do not use discount language."

## Proof / trust signals
Numbers, references, certificates, history. Credibility in the copy comes from these.
Example: "Since 2018 · 4,200 families · certified organic · Aegean coast."
