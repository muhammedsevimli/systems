# 01 · Service

> Fill this file in with your own work. The system reads it on every run; the more specific you are, the more accurate the messages.
> A filled-in example: `brand/EXAMPLE-01-service.md`.

## Who you are / what you do
In one sentence: what you do, what you sell.

## For whom (ideal client)
Who you serve. Industry, size, revenue/budget range, the client with which problem.

## What you sell (packages + real price range)
- Package 1: <name> · <what it includes> · <price range>
- Package 2: <name> · <what it includes> · <price range>
- If you have a smallest unit of work (a trial package / a one-off job), write it.

> The price range matters: the system will not put a prospect whose budget does not fit your range at the top.

## Which problems you solve (weak point mapping)
The system maps the weak point in a prospect's ad to your service. Write which weak point you can solve:
- weak hook (the first sentence does not stop anyone): <do you solve it, how>
- unclear offer (you cannot tell what you get): <do you solve it, how>
- single format (all the same type of visual, no testing): <do you solve it, how>
- no social proof (no testimonial, number, or review): <do you solve it, how>

## Proof / why you
Your real work example, duration, result. The system does not write made-up results; it uses whatever is here, and leaves a placeholder when there is nothing.

## What you are NOT
Your boundary. E.g. "I do not send bulk cold email. I do not offer work I do not do. I do not promise guaranteed results."
