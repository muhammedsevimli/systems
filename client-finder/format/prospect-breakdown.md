# Prospect Breakdown Anatomy + Ranking Score

## Break down for each prospect
- **Brand:** the name.
- **What they sell:** product/service, one sentence.
- **For whom:** the audience the ad speaks to.
- **How long they have been running ads:** from the "started running on" date in the library. This is the BUDGET SIGNAL.
- **How many different ads:** how many variants are running at once. Many variants = serious budget + a testing culture.
- **Format:** single image / video / carousel / mixed.
- **Weak point:** which of the four below (by showing it from the ad).

## The weak point four (this is the anchor of the message)
1. **Weak hook:** the first sentence opens with the brand name or a generic line, and stops no one. Symptom: "As X brand...", "Our quality products...".
2. **Unclear offer:** after reading the ad you cannot tell what you get, for how much, or why now. Symptom: a "Learn More" button, no price/package/result in the copy.
3. **Single format:** all the same type of visual, the same creative for months. It means there is no testing. Symptom: 1 variant, 6 months, the same visual.
4. **No social proof:** no testimonial, number, customer sentence, or before/after. Symptom: the copy talks only about itself.

> Do not write the weak point in GENERAL terms. "The hook is weak" is not enough; show the sentence from the ad: "the first sentence is 'Meet our quality products', that will not stop anyone."

## Ranking score (0-10 · three axes)
Score each prospect on three axes, add them up, and rank from high to low.

**Axis 1 · Budget signal (0-4)**
- 4: 6+ months of uninterrupted ads, more than one variant.
- 3: 3-6 months of ads.
- 2: 1-3 months of ads.
- 1: less than a month, a single ad (may be the test phase).
- 0: the ads have stopped.

**Axis 2 · How solvable the weakness is with your service (0-4)**
Map it against the "which problems you solve" section of `brand/01-service.md`.
- 4: the prospect's main weak point is exactly what you solve.
- 2-3: partly matching, solvable with a secondary service of yours.
- 0-1: they have a weak point, but it is not your work. (Write this honestly, do not inflate it.)

**Axis 3 · Fit with the price range (0-2)**
- 2: the ad volume/business size fits the price range in `brand/01-service.md`.
- 1: borderline.
- 0: too small or too big (enterprise, has its own team).

**Total:** 8-10 = write to this one first. 5-7 = second round. 0-4 = do not write, keep it on the list, write the reason.

## Ranking output
A table: rank · brand · budget signal · main weak point · score (with the axis breakdown) · why it is in that position (one line).
Never give a score without a reason. Transparency is credibility; if the user does not agree with the ranking, they should be able to see the reasoning and make their own call.

## Honest limit (the access model)
The Meta Ad Library is public and free; you can see every active ad from your browser. However, there is no free automatic download (API) for commercial product ads. Meta's public API covers only political and social-issue ads, not commercial ones. That is why the step of SEEING a brand is a one-click "open and paste" job, not automatic scraping. There are third-party tools that scrape the library automatically, but they are paid and their terms of use are debatable; this system does not require them.
The breakdown, budget signal reading, scoring, ranking, and message generation are fully automatic.
