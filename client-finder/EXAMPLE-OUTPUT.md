## (c) Tailored first messages

### 1 · Kökboya Home Textiles (DM · casual)

hi Kökboya, in the duvet ad that has been running for 7 months, the first sentence opens with "As Kökboya, we are here with our quality home textile products". openings that start with the brand name usually get scrolled past in the second second.

I would try a variant that builds the first sentence from the customer's own problem, with the same visual, changing only the opening line and the button goal. put the two side by side and you see which one lands within a week.

want me to write two variants and send them over?

`basis:` 3 single-image variants running for 7 months; the first sentence opens with the brand name, the button is "Learn More".
`weak point:` weak hook + unclear offer.
`why they might reply:` they have been running the same ad for a long time; the suggestion is small, free, and a week's work, so the bar is low.

---

### 2 · Slipper World (DM · casual)

hi Slipper World, in the booties ad that has been running for 5 months the visual has never changed, a single variant. the "Do your feet get cold in winter" hook and the "Buy 2 get 1 free" offer are well built, there is no problem there.

the one issue is that a winter hook is running in July right now, and there is no second variant next to it to compare. I would turn the same offer into a second variant with a summer-appropriate hook and test them side by side.

if I put together a summer hook variant, would you try it?

`basis:` a single image unchanged for 5 months; a winter hook running in mid-July.
`weak point:` single format (no testing).
`why they might reply:` I acknowledge their hook and offer first, and only point at the missing test; the suggestion is cheap to try.

---

### 3 · Nar Natural Cosmetics (DM · casual)

hi Nar, you have been running four different variants for 6 months, and in one of the videos the founder speaks for herself. you have already built the testing side well, I would not touch it.

the one thing I noticed is that the ads talk entirely about the product, and none of them has a customer sentence or a rating. you have the "30-day return" trust in place, and next to it I would turn one happy customer sentence into copy and add a social proof variant. I would not collect new reviews, I would only turn what you already have into an ad.

do you have a customer message I could use?

`basis:` 4 variants over 6 months, multiple formats; none has a customer review, rating, or user video.
`weak point:` no social proof.
`why they might reply:` I acknowledge their work first, point at one clear gap, and am honest about the limit of what I do (I do not collect reviews).

---

### 4 · Zeyn Jewelry (DM · casual) · second round

hi Zeyn, in the necklace video you have been running for two weeks, the "Do you remember the necklace your mother wore" opening really does stop people, your hook is strong. the only gap is that there is no second variant to compare right now, and the "Discover Now" button leaves what happens next a little unclear.

I would keep the hook exactly as it is, turn the button into a clear action, and test it side by side with a second opening. which one gets more clicks becomes obvious in two weeks.

if I wrote a second opening variant for the same video, would you want to try it?

`basis:` a 2-week-old single video; strong hook, unclear "Discover Now" button.
`weak point:` unclear offer (button) + single format.
`why they might reply:` I praise their hook and suggest a small, concrete improvement. Warning: the brand is 2 weeks old and the budget signal is still weak; if no reply comes, put the energy into ranks 1-3.

## (d) Platform note
The four messages above are written for Instagram/DM in a direct, casual register.
If you go through LinkedIn or email, build the same message in a more measured register: "want me to write two variants" becomes "would you like me to put together two variants", and "would you try it" becomes "would you be open to trying it". The content, the basis, and the one-question rule stay the same; only the register changes.
