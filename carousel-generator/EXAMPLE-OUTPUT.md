# Why a Weekly Bread Subscription Makes Sense

Platform: Instagram (informal "you") · 7 slides · Meydan Bakery
Source: brand + voice + swipe + format files

---

## 3 Hooks (the user picks one)

**Hook 1 · Pain angle**
"by the time you are up, the fresh bread is always gone."

**Hook 2 · Number angle**
"decide once, get fresh bread for 7 mornings."

**Hook 3 · Contrarian angle** _(suggested for the cover · swipe: pricing topic → contrarian)_
"a subscription is assumed to be the priciest route. the math says the opposite."

---

## Slides

### Slide 1 · Cover
a subscription is assumed to be the priciest route.
the math says the opposite.

visual: dark stone-oven background, the line "the math says the opposite" stands out with big typography and a single accent color.

### Slide 2 · Pain
you are late in the morning, the fresh bread is already gone.
on the days you cannot stop by, only the evening batch-baked bread is left.
the same thing every week.

visual: an emptied bread basket on the morning counter, a single ordinary loaf left.

### Slide 3 · Why it happens
fresh bread comes out early in the morning and sells out fast.
you are rushing to work at exactly that hour.
that is why you always get "the leftovers".

visual: a wall clock showing 07:00, freshly baked bread behind it.

### Slide 4 · Core of the solution
a subscription handles one thing: your bread is set aside in your name in the morning.
even if you are late, even if you cannot stop by that day, it waits for you.

visual: a single loaf in a paper bag with a name tag, a "reserved" note.

### Slide 5 · How it works
you sign up on the weekly list once.
every morning your fresh bread is set aside from the stone oven.
we have been doing this since 2019 for 60+ families.

visual: a hand-written weekly subscriber notebook, day-by-day marks next to the names.

### Slide 6 · Result / gain
buying one at a time costs you both the trip and the time, and sometimes you end up with no bread.
with a subscription the math is fixed: [put your own weekly price here] a week, fresh every morning.
no rushing, no bread worries.

visual: two squares side by side, on the left "one at a time" scattered receipts, on the right "subscription" a single clear number.

### Slide 7 · Close + CTA
let us set aside your weekly bread in your name every morning.
write "subscribe" in the comments, we will add you to the list.

visual: a plain light background, the Meydan Bakery logo and a single "subscribe" button feel.
