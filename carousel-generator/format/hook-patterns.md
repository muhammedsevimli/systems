# Hook Patterns · Formula Bank

> When the system produces 3 hooks for each carousel it picks three different patterns from this bank.
> The patterns are the skeleton; brand + topic fill them in. None of them carry a motivational or hype tone.

## Rule
- 3 hooks per carousel: one PAIN angle, one NUMBER angle, one CONTRARIAN angle.
- The cover slide is at most a 6-8 word main line. If it is long, cut it.
- Do not give the payoff on the cover. Leave curiosity/tension, make them swipe.
- Banned: "last chance", "do not miss out", exclamation storms, "amazing/awesome", empty adjectives, em dash.

## 1. Pain angle (safest, most frequent)
Put the reader's struggle on the cover.
- "[target audience] is still [doing the wrong/tiring thing]."
- "every [time/situation] [recurring pain]."
- "you do [work] but [expected result] does not come."
Example: "you recalculate the price from scratch for every new client."

## 2. Number angle (concrete, a sense of proof)
Create curiosity with a number. The number must be real; if not, do not use this pattern.
- "[number] [thing], in [short time]."
- "[loss] because of [number] mistakes."
- "the only thing you need for [big job]: [number] [small thing]."
Example: "3 slides, 15 minutes, zero design software."

## 3. Contrarian angle (in moderation, 1 per post)
A measured objection to what everyone assumes is right. No guru tone, an observation tone.
- "[common advice] does not work. here is why."
- "you do not need [popular thing]."
- "[the thing assumed certain] is not always true."
Example: "giving a discount might be pushing the client away."

## 4. Curiosity gap (backup pattern)
- "we have been doing [thing] wrong for [time]."
- "nobody tells you this about [topic]."
- "the one thing behind [result]."

## Selection logic
- If the topic is a STRUGGLE: pain + contrarian are strong.
- If the topic is a METHOD: number + curiosity gap are strong.
- The system produces all three, the user picks one by platform.
