# Carousel Format · Slide Anatomy

> The system produces every carousel to this structure. The slide count and each slide's job are fixed here.
> The goal: each slide does one job, the viewer stops the thumb and swipes to the end.

## How many slides
- Standard: 7 slides (cover + 5 body + close). Ideal for Instagram, neither too short nor tiring.
- If the topic is narrow it can drop to 5 (cover + 3 body + close); if broad it can go to 9. Do not exceed 9.
- One idea per slide. If you are fitting two ideas into one slide, split it.

## Slide-by-slide jobs

### Slide 1 · Cover (scroll-stopper)
- One job: stop the thumb. The chosen hook goes here.
- Short, big, clear. At most a 6-8 word main line + one sub line.
- Spark curiosity or pain; do not give the payoff, leave a "swipe" feeling.
- visual: strongest contrast here; big typography, a single accent color.

### Slide 2 · Pain / situation
- Name the real struggle the reader lives. Make them say "yes, that is me".
- 2-3 short sentences. Be concrete, do not go generic.

### Slide 3 · Why it happens / what is going wrong
- The root cause of the struggle, or the mistake everyone makes.
- One idea. A "that is why" logic.

### Slide 4 · Core of the solution
- Your approach, an idea caught in one sentence. The heart of the carousel.
- An angle that landed from the swipe-file can go here.

### Slide 5 · How / steps
- 2-3 applicable steps or one clear example. If there is a concrete number, here.
- If you need proof, from the brand proof line; otherwise a placeholder, no inventing.

### Slide 6 · Result / gain
- What changes when you do this. One clear gain sentence.
- No motivational language, a concrete benefit.

### Slide 7 · Close + CTA
- One clear call to action: "save", "write X in the comments", "check the link in the profile".
- ONE CTA per post. Do not put two calls.
- visual: plain, brand logo + a single button feel.

## Text rules (every slide)
- 2-3 short sentences per slide. No long paragraphs; it is read on a phone.
- Sentences in spoken language, straight to the point. No filler adjectives.
- If there is a number, write it (as a numeral, not spelled out): "3 steps", "15 minutes".
- A "visual:" line under every slide: what should appear on that slide, one sentence of direction.

## Platform note
- Instagram: informal "you", a 1080x1350 vertical slide assumption.
- Translating to LinkedIn: same slides, a more measured tone. The system gives this translation at the end.
