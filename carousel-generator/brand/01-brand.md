# 01 · Brand

> The unchanging information behind the carousel: who, for whom, what we sell, what we are not, proof.
> The system builds every carousel from this file. Fill it once, it rarely changes.
> If you already set up a brand memory or second brain, this file is the same as the 01 file there, copy it.

## Who / what we do
What you do in one sentence.
Example: "A one-person studio that sets up social media order and content for small businesses."

## For whom (target audience)
Who you speak to. Age, industry, pain, lifestyle. Carousels are written for this person.
Example: "small business owners and service providers who run their own shop and have no time to make content."

## What you sell (offers)
- Offer 1: <name> · <who it fits>
- Offer 2: <name> · <who it fits>

## Why you (differentiation)
The thing only you can say, that your competitor cannot. The carousel's "core of the solution" slide feeds from here.
Example: "we set up every piece not one by one but as a monthly plan; the client never has to think."

## What you are NOT
The boundary. When the system reads these lines it will not produce the wrong angle.
Example: "we are not an ad agency. we do not promise viral followers. we do not use discount language."

## Proof / trust signals
Numbers, references, track record. Credibility on the "how" slide comes from these.
The system does NOT put made-up numbers; it uses whatever is on this line, and leaves a placeholder if there is nothing.
Example: "since 2022 · 30+ businesses · a steady content flow in about 6 weeks on average."
