# 01 · Brand

> The unchanging information behind the sales angle: who, for whom, what we sell, what we are not, proof.
> The system builds objection responses and sales angles from this file. Fill it once, it rarely changes.
> If you already set up a brand memory, carousel generator, or ad spy, this file is the same as the 01 file there, copy it.

## Who / what we do
What you do, what you sell, in one sentence.
Example: "A small e-commerce brand selling natural skincare: moisturizer, face oil, hand cream."

## For whom (target audience)
Who you speak to. Age, pain, lifestyle, reason for buying. Angles are written for this person.
Example: "women 25-45 with dry skin, avoiding chemicals, looking for natural and gentle products; mostly shopping online."

## What you sell (offers + real numbers)
Write your product/offer names and, IF you have them, your real offers. The system does not invent a discount/offer that is not here.
- Offer 1: <name> · <price range / size>
- Offer 2: <name> · <price range / size>
- Your first-order / shipping / return terms (write the real one if you have it, otherwise leave blank).

## Why you (differentiation)
The thing only you can say, that your competitor cannot. The promise part of the sales angles feeds from here.
Example: "every product is small-batch, additive-free; prepared in our own kitchen; a hand-written usage note in every order."

## What you are NOT
The boundary. When the system reads these lines it will not blindly turn a request in a review into an angle.
Example: "we do not do cheap-race pricing. we do not make 'miracle cream' promises. we do not claim medical/dermatological treatment."

## Proof / trust signals
Numbers, references, guarantees, track record. Credibility on the objection response and the hook's proof line comes from these.
The system does NOT put made-up numbers; it uses whatever is on this line, and leaves a placeholder if there is nothing.
Example: "since 2020 · 6,000+ orders · 14-day returns · 4.7 store rating."

## My real responses to common objections (optional but very useful)
When a customer gets hung up on something, what is your honest response? The system builds the objection response first from here, then from other customers' words.
Example:
- my response to "the jar is small": "a hazelnut-sized amount covers the whole face, one jar lasts about 2 months on average. there is also a 100 ml size for those who want it."
- my response to "expensive": "additive-free, small-batch production; we do not put cheap filler inside, so this is the price."
