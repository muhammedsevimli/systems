# Sales Arsenal · Output Format

> The system writes each analysis to the `outputs/` folder in this structure. Sections in order.

## Structure of the output file
A) Scan summary.
B) Theme clusters (three groups).
C) The most common objection.
D) Sales arsenal (per theme: angle + objection response + hook).
E) Proof trail.
F) No-invention note.
G) Platform note (Instagram "you", LinkedIn measured register).

## A) Scan summary
- How many reviews scanned, what the source is (Trendyol / Instagram / mixed).
- Sentiment split: how many positive, how many mixed, how many criticism.
- The overall picture in one sentence: what the customer loves most, what trips them up most.

## B) Theme clusters (three groups)
Under each group the themes, WITH how many reviews each appears in and a real customer quote:

### 1. Why they buy (reasons for buying)
- <theme> · N reviews · "real customer sentence"

### 2. What they trust (trust factors)
- <theme> · N reviews · "real customer sentence"

### 3. What holds them back (objections)
- <theme> · N reviews · "real customer sentence"

## C) The most common objection
- The objection in one sentence, how many reviews it appears in, why it matters (must be answered up front on the sales page).
- If there is one, the real customer sentence that refutes this objection (a ready response).

## D) Sales arsenal
For the 4-6 most common themes (first the most common objection, then the strongest reasons for buying):

### Theme N · <short name>
- **sales angle:** <which benefit you bring forward, in the customer's own words>
- **objection response:** <the up-front answer to the hesitation around this theme; in another customer's words if possible; otherwise the brand's real offer; otherwise a placeholder>
- **ad hook:** <1-2 scroll-stopping opening sentences, in the customer's language>
- **source:** <which reviews it came from, a short quote>

## E) Proof trail
The list of real reviews each important angle rests on (short quotes). The reader should be able to say "this is not invented, my customer said so".

## F) No-invention note
- State in one line which common claims were NOT written because they have no basis in the reviews (e.g. "no timeframe promise was made because 'how many days to take effect' was not stated clearly in the reviews").
- List the placeholders left for numbers/offers not in the brand file.

## G) Platform note
- All hooks and angles are in the "you" form for Instagram / product page.
- The LinkedIn measured-register version: 2-3 examples + the rule (verbs and address stay measured, ease off the emotion a touch, bring concrete proof to the front).

## Hard rules (summary)
- Do not invent a benefit the customer did not mention; every angle's source is a real review.
- Do not carry a competitor/other brand's words; build from your own customer's language.
- No made-up numbers/discounts/guarantees, otherwise a placeholder.
- Tone from `02-voice.md`. No banned words. No em dashes.
